
SKITTLES-BASED GAMES

Name: Devil among the tailors
What: Tabletop skittles where nine pins on a square table are knocked down by 
a ball swung around a pole
Where: Bedfordshire, Cambridge, Macclesfield
Pubs: The Cock at Broom <http://www.thecockatbroom.co.uk/>, the Queens Head 
<http://www.telegraph.co.uk/foodanddrink/pubs/3318556/Pint-to-pint-Queens-Head.html>
 in Newton, Cambridge,the Wharf 
<http://thewharfmacc.co.uk/?ai1ec_event=macclesfield-skittle-league-presentation>
, Macclesfield

Name: Dadlums
What: Tabletop skittles in which nine pins are knocked down by cheeses
Where: Kent
Pubs: The Jolly Drayman <http://www.jollydrayman.com/>, Gravesend

Name: Alley skittles 
What: Nine pins are knocked down by a ball, three throws per turn
Where: All over the West Country, Wales, Scotland, Staffordshire 
Pubs: The Hawbridge Inn <http://www.hawbridgeinn.co.uk>, Gloucestershire, the 
Railway Inn <http://www.railwayinnwestbury.co.uk/>, Westbury, the Old Cross 
Inn, Cardiff, the Otter Inn <http://www.otterinn.co.uk/>, east Devon, the Blue 
Anchor Inn <http://www.spingoales.com/>, Helston, The Frampton Arms 
<http://framptonarms.co.uk/>, Dorset, the Sheep Heid Inn 
<http://www.sheepheid.co.uk/location/>, Edinburgh, the Burton Bridge Inn 
<http://www.burtonbridgeinn.co.uk/index.htm>, Burton on Trent

Name: Long alley skittles
What: Balls or cheeses are thrown at nine pins. The projectile must bounce 
once, and the kingpin must be hit first
Where: East Midlands
Pubs: The Horse and Groom <http://www.horseandgroomqueniborough>, 
Leicestershire, the Clock Warehouse <http://www.clockwarehousepub.co.uk/>, 
Derbyshire

Name: Aunt Sally
 What: Players throw wooden skittles at a wooden baton known as a doll
Where: Oxfordshire <http://oxfordauntsally.co.uk/venues.php> 
Pubs: Lots in the county, including the Crown Inn <http://crownsydenham.com/>, 
Sydenham

CARD-BASED GAMES

 Name: Euchre
What: A trick-taking card game most commonly played with four people in two 
partnerships, with a deck of 24 or 32 cards
Where: St Austell, Cornwall
Pubs: The Sawles Arms 
<http://www.staustellbrewery.co.uk/pub/carthew/sawles-arms>

Name: Cribbage
What: A card game that involves playing and grouping cards in combinations 
that gain points, with a board for score-keeping
Where: Norwich
Pubs: The Duke of Wellington <http://www.dukeofwellingtonnorwich.co.uk/> (find 
more at cribbageinnorwich.co.uk)

WEIRD TABLE GAMES

Name: Nine men’s morris
What: A strategy board game in which players try to form “mills” – three of 
their own pieces in a line
Where: Kent
Pubs: The Red Lion, Snargate

Name: Bar billiards
 What: Players strike balls up the table with a cue, attempting to pot them 
without knocking over mushrooms, against the clock
Where: Sheffield
Pubs: The Cross Scythes <http://www.crossscythes.co.uk/>, the Gardeners Rest 
<http://www.sheffieldbrewery.com/gardeners-rest.php> and the New Barrack Tavern 
<http://newbarracktavern.com/>

Name: Bagatelle
 What: Players use cues to shoot balls into holes at the rounded end of the 
board
Where: Chester
Pubs: The Olde Cottage Inn <http://www.oldecottagechester.co.uk/>, the Cross 
Foxes <http://officialpubguide.com/pubdetails.php?pubid=6389>, the Spital Vaults
 <http://www.beerintheevening.com/pubs/s/30/30038/Spital_Vaults/Chester>

PENNY GAMES

Name: Shove ha’penny
 What: Players push coins up a board, aiming to get a coin in each “bed” three 
times
Where: Dorset and Somerset
Pubs: The Railway <http://www.railwayblandford.com/>, Blandford, the Three 
Swans <http://www.beerintheevening.com/pubs/s/35/35883/Three_Swans_Inn/Frome>, 
Frome

Name: Pitch penny/Nurdles
 What: Players throw pennies across the room into a hole carved into a 
high-backed bench
Where: Stretton, Rutland
Pubs: The Jackson Stops Inn <http://www.thejacksonstops.com/index.html>

Name: Push penny
 What: Similar to shove ha’penny, but using larger old English pennies
Where: Stamford, Lincolnshire
Pubs: The Jolly Brewer <http://jollybrewer.com/>, the Tobie Norris 
<http://www.kneadpubs.co.uk/our-pubs/the-tobie-norris>

Name: Toad in the hole
 What: Similar to pitch penny, but brass discs, or “toads”, are thrown into a 
hole in a table or box
Where: Lewes, East Sussex
Pubs: The Trevor Arms <http://trevorarms.com/>, the Brewers Arms 
<http://www.brewersarmslewes.co.uk/>

LOOPY-HOOPY GAMES

 Name: Ringing the bull
 What: Players swing a metal ring on a rope on to a metal hook, perhaps in the 
nose of a bull’s head
Where: Nottingham and Warwickshire
Pubs: Ye Olde Trip to Jerusalem <http://triptojerusalem.com/>, Nottingham, the 
White Hart Inn, <http://www.whitehartnewbold.co.uk/> Stratford-upon-Avon

Name: Quoits
 What: Rings are thrown at target “hobs” or stakes
Where: Esk Valley, North Yorkshire
Pubs: The Black Horse Inn <http://www.blackhorseinnkirkbyfleetham.com/>

GROUP GAMES

Name: Dwile flonking
What: Two teams take turns to dance around each other while attempting to 
avoid a beer-soaked cloth, or “dwile”
Where: East Anglia, Sussex
Pubs: The Dog Inn <http://www.thedoginnludham.co.uk/>, Ludham Bridge,  
<http://www.geldestonlocks.co.uk/>the Lewes Arms <http://lewesarms.co.uk/>

Name: Bat and trap
 What: A twist on cricket. Batters hit a wooden see-saw with a paddle to 
release the ball, then bat it to the bowler
Where: Sevenoaks, Kent
Pubs: The White Rock Inn, <http://www.thewhiterockinn.co.uk/> Ye Olde 
Beverlie, Canterbury. <http://www.yeoldebeverlie.com/> More pubs here 
<http://www.batandtrap.org.uk/?page_id=35>

Name: Dominoes
 What: Players try to empty their hands of numbered, rectangular tiles while 
blocking their opponents
Where: Glasgow
Pubs: The Cornerstone <http://www.thecornerstoneglasgow.co.uk/home-8999.html)>,
The Old Oak <http://www.oldoakinnrutherglen.co.uk/home-9961.html>
