
Stratford-upon-Avon

New attractions to mark the big occasion include the Other Place 
<https://www.rsc.org.uk/your-visit/the-other-place>, an RSC studio theatre that 
has been closed for nearly 10 years. It holds talks by actors and directors, 
and visitors can look inside the costume store and climb up the tower (tours 
£8.50/£4.50, tower climb from £1). Shakespeare’s Schoolroom 
<http://www.shakespearesschoolroom.org/> is a new immersive attraction bringing 
16th-century Stratford to life (opens 23 April). TheSwan Wing 
<https://www.rsc.org.uk/about-us/transforming-our-theatres>, the oldest part of 
the RSC’s theatres, is reopening after restoration with a new exhibition 
telling the stories behind the productions (exhibition opens in June).
Shakespeare’s New Place 
<http://www.shakespeare.org.uk/about-us/new-place-the-next-chapter.html>, the 
site of his house for 19 years (long since demolished), is reopening as a 
heritage landmark. There will be landscaped gardens, artworks and an exhibition 
at Nash’s House next door (opens in July).

On 23 April, the Bard’s birthday and also the 400th anniversary of his death, 
there is a jazz procession by theNew Orleans Shakespeare festival 
<http://neworleansshakespeare.org/>, street entertainment and a Shakespeare 
Show broadcast live from the Royal Shakespeare Theatre, followed by fireworks (
shakespearescelebrations.com <https://www.shakespearescelebrations.com/>). 
There is also aliterary festival <http://www.stratfordliteraryfestival.co.uk> 
(24 April to 1 May) and apoetry festival <http://www.shakespeare.org.uk> in 
July.Shakespeare’s Steps <http://www.shakespeare.org.uk> is a year-long 
interactive installation across town, with mini scenes from his life and works.
Holy Trinity church 
<http://www.stratford-upon-avon.org/events/calendar/?y=2016&m=april>, where 
Shakespeare was baptised and buried, is hosting several musical tributes this 
month, including opera and an ode by poet Carol Ann Duffy. TheWorld Shakespeare 
Congress <http://www.wsc2016.info> is the world’s greatest gathering of 
Shakespeareans (31 July to 6 August).

Shakespeare’s Globe is staging the Complete Walk 
<http://www.shakespearesglobe.com/theatre/whats-on/special-events/the-complete-walk>
: 37 new short films on 37 screens on a 2.5-mile route along the Thames between 
Westminster and Tower Bridges. Each film explores an aspect of one of 
Shakespeare’s plays, was shot on location – Macbeth at Glamis Castle, 
Coriolanus in Rome – and stars actors including Dominic West, Gemma Arterton 
and Toby Jones (23-24 April, free). There areShakespeare cruises 
<http://www.citycruises.com/city-cruises-sightseeing-thames-circular.aspx> on 
the Thames this month, with actors performing sonnets, sketches and scenes, 
including a montage of Shakespearean insults (from £13). TheV&A Performance 
festival 
<https://shop.vam.ac.uk/whatson/index/view/id/1804/event/V-A-Performance-Festival-2016/dt/2016-04-16/free/1>

Night, plus a poetry marathon of all 154 sonnets, and responses to them by 154 
contemporary poets (ends 24 April). TheShakespeare in Shoreditch festival 
<http://www.shakespeareinshoreditch.in> includes a world record attempt by a 
company touring Hackney to perform 1,000 plays in 10 days (runs to 30 April). 
Richard II is being performed in theHouse of Commons

to theRegent’s Park Open Air Theatre <http://www.openairtheatre.com> (17 June-9 
July).

Bristol

Bristol’s annual, volunteer-run celebration of the bard lasts for a month and 
will feature performances outdoors and in unusual locations. Last year, for 
example, The Tempest was staged in Leigh Woods nature reserve and Macbeth in 
the atmospheric setting of the Redcliffe Caves. The programme and tickets for 
the events (30 June-31 July) go on sale on 23 April.

St Albans

This ambitious festival of 31 performances in a dozen locations across the 
city takes in all 37 Shakespeare plays. On the anniversary (23 April) various 
scenes from the plays will be performed at local landmarks, and the festival 
culminates with Ralph Vaughan Williams’ Serenade to Music – as part of an 
evening of Shakespeare-inspired choral music and readings presented by Mosaic 
Chamber Choir and the OVO theatre company at the city’s cathedral (4 June).
• stalbansshakespeare.co.uk <http://stalbansshakespeare.co.uk/>

Belfast 

Two hundred volunteer actors, dancers, singers and drummers are joining a 
professional international team for this epic show that spans 150 years of 
history, from Belfast’s 19th-century shipping greatness to its modern 
intercultural future. Filling a former shipyard warehouse (T13) on Queen’s 
Island in the Titanic Quarter, the immersive show features live music and 
holographic surround sound. Bring a cushion, warm clothes (the space is 
unheated) and maybe a picnic, and take your place on a raft or shipwreck 
surrounded by sand and water (until April 23).
• terranovaproductions.ne 
<http://www.terranovaproductions.net/belfast-tempest-box/>
