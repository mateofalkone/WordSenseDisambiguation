
A headline, “This man is worth £75m. He makes money from providing services to 
his own state-run academies.Are England’s schools becoming a cash cow for 
businessmen? 
<http://www.theguardian.com/education/2016/jun/12/academy-schools-cash-cow-business>
” appeared last week on an investigation into academy trust funding and next to 
a photograph of the businessman Mike Dwan (News, page 8). Dr Dwan, a benefactor 
to Bright Tribe Trust and Adventure Learning Academy Trust, has asked us to 
point out that neither he nor any of his associated trusts or companies make 
any profit from their work for the academies they support. The trusts’ annual 
reports and financial statements show that he and his companies ensure that the 
trusts are fully funded and, where necessary, are provided with services at 
cost or less, in compliance with the Academies Financial Handbook.

An error introduced into a story headlined “Third of EU referendum voters 
won’t make up their mind until week before poll 
<http://www.theguardian.com/politics/2016/jun/11/brexit-eu-referendum-vote-last-minute-decision-decide-lse>
” (News, last week, page 7) made it imply that 93% of Ukip supporters would 
wish to remain in the European Union and 5% leave. We meant exactly the 
opposite.
