
• An article about attacks on British politicians described Edgar Graham as an 
Ulster Unionist party MP. The law lecturer, who was shot dead in 1983, was a 
member of the Northern Ireland assembly, not the Westminster parliament (
Attacks on MPs: From Irish republicans to a student radicalised online 
<https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2016/jun/16/jo-cox-attack-latest-serious-assault-mps>
, 17 June, page 4).

• The first step in a recipe for cardamom cake told readers to “grease and 
flour a loaf tin”. To clarify, it should be a large loaf tin or a 24cm bundt 
tin (Readers’ recipe swap: Cardamom 
<http://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2016/jun/11/readers-recipe-swap-cardamom-dale-berning-sawa>
, 11 June, page 12, Cook).

• We misspelled the forename of Yuko Takahashi as Yuki in a review of the 
restaurant Takahashi, in south Wimbledon 
<http://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2016/jun/10/takahashi-london-sw19-restaurant-review-japanese-marina-oloughlin>
 (11 June, page 75, Weekend).

• Homophone corner: “We need to replace both capitalism and socialism with an 
agreed principal of global management…” (Potential benefits and pitfalls of a 
universal basic income 
<http://www.theguardian.com/politics/2016/jun/10/potential-benefits-and-pitfalls-of-a-universal-basic-income>
, 11 June, page 34).
