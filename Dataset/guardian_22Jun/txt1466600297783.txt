
Raised in Sussex, Richard Osman 
<http://www.theguardian.com/profile/richard-osman>, 45, is a television writer 
who helped develop the format forthe BBC quiz show Pointless 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2013/jun/04/pointless-alexander-armstrong-richard-osman>
 and, in 2009, found himself cast as co-host with Alexander Armstrong. His 
other work includes presentingTwo Tribes 
<http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b04fhqq0> and appearances on Have I Got News 
For You <http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b006mkw3> and QI 
<http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b006ml0g>; he is a contestant on the new 
series ofTaskmaster <http://dave.uktv.co.uk/shows/taskmaster/> on Dave. He is 
divorced, has two children and lives in London.

When were you happiest?
Probably when Fulham beat Juventus 4-1 in the Europa League 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2010/mar/18/fulham-juventus-europa-league>
.

What is your greatest fear?
Putting the bins out too late.

What is the trait you most deplore in yourself?
My sneezes sound like coughs.

What is the trait you most deplore in others?
I hate this new tendency to think that anyone who disagrees with you is stupid 
or part of some conspiracy.

Aside from a property, what’s the most expensive thing you’ve bought?
Coconut water from Whole Foods Market.

What would your super power be?
There’s only one right answer to this and it is teleportation.

What makes you unhappy?
My propensity to unhappiness.

What do you most dislike about your appearance?
I have no idea how to dress properly.

Who would play you in the film of your life?
Ben Whishaw sitting on Eddie Redmayne’s shoulders.

What is the worst thing anyone’s said to you?
When I was 10 and my dad called us into the living room and told us he was 
leaving. No one has managed to top that. A big part of me is still in that room.

What is your favourite smell?
I like that slightly smoky smell that tells you autumn is on its way and all 
the guys in the park will start putting their tops back on soon.

What did you want to be when you were growing up?
A police officer, like my grandad.

What is your guiltiest pleasure?
I do sometimes find myself watching Judge Rinder on ITV.

What do you owe your parents?
To my mum, everything.

To whom would you most like to say sorry, and why?
To my kids, for my shortcomings.

What or who is the greatest love of your life?
She knows. That should cover it.

What does love feel like?
Like you’re in the best gang in town.

Have you ever said ‘I love you’ without meaning it?
No, but I have said “I love this asparagus tart,” “I love your tattoo” and “I 
love Radiohead” without meaning it.

Who would you invite to your dream dinner party?
Terry Wogan, Ronnie Corbett and Victoria Wood, please.

When did you last cry, and why?
I cry at everything – adverts, Gogglebox, hugging my kids. I love crying: it’s 
like your pain is laughing.

What single thing would improve the quality of your life?
The capacity to genuinely enjoy going to the gym.

What do you consider your greatest achievement?
My children.

What song would you like played at your funeral?
The Man Comes Around <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=k9IfHDi-2EA> by Johnny 
Cash, orthe theme tune to Pointless 
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=20TU0qfGqcY>.

What is the most important lesson life has taught you?
It’s nice to be important, but it’s more important to be nice.
