
Born in Berkhamsted in 1940, Rantzen 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/esther-rantzen> presented the BBC 
television seriesThat’s Life <http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/That's_Life!> for 21 
years from 1973. She foundedChildline 
<http://www.childline.org.uk/Pages/Home.aspx> in 1986 and The Silver Line  
<http://www.thesilverline.org.uk>in 2013, and was made a dame for services to 
children and older people in the2015 New Year honours list 
<https://www.theguardian.com/uk-news/2014/dec/30/new-year-honours-2015-damehoods-rantzen>
. She lives in London and has three children by her late husband, documentary 
makerDesmond Wilcox <http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desmond_Wilcox>.

When were you happiest?
Watching the sun set with Desmond in St Paul de Vence, September 2000.

What is your greatest fear?
Dying.

What is your earliest memory?
Standing in my cot listening to an air-raid siren.

Which living person do you most admire, and why?
Sir Nicholas Winton <http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicholas_Winton>, who saved 
a generation of Czech Jewish children – at the age of 105, he is as original, 
visionary and ornery as ever.

What is the trait you most deplore in yourself?
I cry too easily.

What is the trait you most deplore in others?
Bullying.
