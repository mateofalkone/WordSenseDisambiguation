
For many pop stars, a new haircut is the calling card of individuality. In 
that highly controlled, infantalising environment, where all your needs are 
taken care of, it can be a much-needed from of expression.

Think about Taylor Swift’s recent bleached bob (Debbie Harry via Little J in 
Gossip Girl 
<http://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2016/may/03/met-ball-2016-questions-red-carpet-new-york-zayn-malik-taylor-swift-beyonce>
), a high fashion reinvention with punk aspirations; Justin Bieber’s dreadlocks,
a questionable nod to black culture 
<http://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2016/apr/05/justin-bieber-dreadlocks-cultural-appropriation-black-pride>
; and, of course, Britney Spears’s shaved head from 2007, which seemed like a 
desperate bid for independence.
 Harry Styles’s grownup hairdo. 
Harry Styles’s long locks telegraphed his mental exit from One Direction 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/one-direction>. Wearing his hair like a 
member of an altogether different band (the Rolling Stones) suggested a bedroom 
door-slamming rebellion from the star-making machine.

And now he has had The Chop. His new hair is swept back 
<http://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2016/may/10/pop-culture-style-men-drakes-shearling-peaky-blinders-caps-catwalk-summer-2016>
 and bouffant. We’re told it’sfor a film role about the second world war 
<http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-3590911/GIRL-TOWN-Harry-Styles-short-new-haircut-star-s-new-movie-roles-Second-World-War-Tommy.html>
. More than that, though, it seems to set the scene for a more mature solo 
venture. It’s a look that says: “I’m no longer on my gap year. I’m ready for 
business.”
